🎤 Discover Seoul Through the Lens of K-pop

Seoul isn’t just the capital of South Korea — it’s the global heart of K-pop culture. From entertainment agencies to fan cafés, every corner of the city pulses with music, passion, and fandom. If you’re planning a trip to Korea in 2025, here are the must-visit K-pop landmarks that every fan should experience.

🏢 1️⃣ SM Entertainment Square (Seongsu-dong)

Located in Seoul’s trendy Seongsu district, the new SM Entertainment building is a hotspot for fans of EXO, Red Velvet, NCT, and aespa. Its modern glass façade and open lobby often attract fans hoping to catch a glimpse of their favorite idols heading to practice.

Highlight: SM Town Café and the SM Archive shop for exclusive merchandise.

🏛️ 2️⃣ HYBE Insight (Yongsan)

Managed by HYBE Corporation, the company behind BTS, SEVENTEEN, and TXT, this interactive museum takes you behind the scenes of the K-pop industry. Visitors can enjoy exclusive exhibits, sound experiences, and limited-edition souvenirs.

Tip: Book tickets online as entry slots fill up quickly. English audio guides are available.

☕ 3️⃣ K-Star Road (Cheongdam-dong)

This famous street in Gangnam features large bear-shaped sculptures called “GangnamDol”, each representing a different K-pop group — from Super Junior to BTS. It’s the perfect photo spot for fans exploring Seoul’s luxury district.

Nearby: FNC, JYP, and Cube Entertainment offices are within walking distance.

🎶 4️⃣ Myeongdong & Hongdae Busking Zones

For a taste of Seoul’s grassroots K-pop culture, head to Hongdae or Myeongdong on weekend nights. You’ll find aspiring idols performing dance covers, live singing, and busking sessions that often go viral online.

Tip: These performances are free — but small cash tips or claps are always appreciated!

🛍️ 5️⃣ Myeongdong Underground Shopping Center

Beyond skincare and street food, Myeongdong offers endless K-pop merchandise shops. From official albums and lightsticks to posters and photo cards, this is heaven for collectors. Look for Music Korea and Soundwave branches underground.

📸 6️⃣ COEX Artium (Gangnam)

Once home to the original SM Town complex, COEX Artium still features rotating exhibitions and K-pop pop-up stores. Even after the SM museum moved, the area remains a must-visit for photo ops near the Starfield Library.

🎧 7️⃣ YG Entertainment HQ (Hapjeong)

Blackpink, BIGBANG, and TREASURE fans will want to stop by the YG Entertainment building. While the interior isn’t open to visitors, fans often gather nearby to snap photos of the iconic black tower and café.

Nearby: The Blackpink House filming location and cafés frequently visited by idols.

🎵 8️⃣ KBS Open Hall (Yeouido)

Want to see a live K-pop performance? Apply for tickets to KBS Music Bank, one of Korea’s most famous weekly music shows. The studio is located in Yeouido, and foreigners can apply for entry through tour packages or official channels.

💡 Final Thoughts

Exploring Seoul’s K-pop landmarks is like stepping inside the world of your favorite idols. Whether you’re visiting a company building, cheering at a live show, or collecting merch, every moment connects you deeper to the culture that made Korea’s music global.

So grab your lightstick — Seoul awaits every true K-pop fan in 2025!

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can tourists visit K-pop entertainment buildings?

Yes, visitors can enter areas like SM Town Café, HYBE Insight, and COEX Artium. However, most agency offices restrict internal access for privacy and security reasons.

2. Where can I buy official K-pop merchandise in Seoul?

Official goods are sold at Myeongdong, COEX Artium, and the SMTOWN & HYBE stores. Avoid unofficial stalls that sell unlicensed products.

3. Are K-pop show tickets available for foreigners?

Yes, tourists can apply for tickets to shows like Music Bank, Inkigayo, and M Countdown through official travel partners or fan packages.

4. Is it possible to meet idols at these landmarks?

It’s rare, but fans sometimes spot idols entering or leaving company buildings. Be respectful and avoid crowding restricted areas.
