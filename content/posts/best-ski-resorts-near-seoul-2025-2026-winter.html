Are you planning a winter trip to Korea? Finding the best ski resorts near Seoul for the 2025-2026 winter season is essential for a perfect itinerary. South Korea offers world-class skiing facilities, and many are located just 40 to 90 minutes away from the capital city.

This guide covers the top ski resorts accessible from Seoul, including Vivaldi Park, Konjiam Resort, and Elysian Gangchon, along with transportation tips.

Why Choose Ski Resorts Near Seoul?

Ski resorts in Gyeonggi-do and nearby Gangwon-do offer excellent accessibility for international tourists. You can enjoy a full day of skiing without the need for overnight accommodation. Most resorts provide shuttle buses from major hubs like Myeongdong, Hongdae, and Gangnam.

Which Ski Resort is the Most Popular for Tourists?

Vivaldi Park Ski World (Hongcheon)

Vivaldi Park Ski World is arguably the most famous ski resort for international visitors. It offers frequent free shuttle buses from Seoul, making it incredibly convenient for first-timers.

  • Location: Hongcheon-gun, Gangwon-do (approx. 1.5 hours from Seoul)
  • Best Feature: "Snowy Land" – a massive snow theme park perfect for sledding and photos.
  • Tip: It can get crowded on weekends; a weekday visit is highly recommended.

What is the Closest Ski Resort to Seoul?

Konjiam Resort (Gwangju)

Konjiam Resort is the closest ski destination, located in Gwangju, Gyeonggi-do. It takes only about 40 minutes from Gangnam by car. It is famous for its premium facilities and "Me-Time Pass" system, which allows you to ski for a specific number of hours efficiently.

  • Location: Gwangju-si, Gyeonggi-do
  • Best Feature: Shortest travel time from Seoul and limited daily entry for a pleasant experience.
  • Note: Prices are slightly higher, but the proximity justifies the cost.

Can I Go Skiing by Subway in Korea?

Elysian Gangchon Resort (Chuncheon)

Yes, you can. Elysian Gangchon Resort is unique because it is accessible via the Seoul subway system (Gyeongchun Line) and the ITX-Cheongchun train.

  • Location: Chuncheon-si, Gangwon-do (approx. 1 hour via ITX)
  • Best Feature: Direct access to Baegyang-ri Station and beginner-friendly slopes.
  • Vibe: Very youthful and popular among university students and independent travelers.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

When does the 2025-2026 ski season start in Korea?

The ski season in Korea typically begins in early December 2025 and continues until early March 2026. The best snow conditions are usually found in January.

Do I need to bring my own ski gear?

No. All major resorts near Seoul offer rental services for skis, snowboards, boots, and clothing (jackets/pants). You should bring your own gloves and goggles, or purchase them at the resort.

How much is a ski lift pass in Korea?

Lift passes vary by duration, but a 4-hour pass typically costs between 60,000 KRW and 90,000 KRW (approx. $45-$70 USD) depending on the resort and discounts.
