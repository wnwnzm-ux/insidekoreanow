Why Nami Island Should Be on Every First-Timer's Korea Itinerary

If you've spent any time scrolling through Korea travel photos, you've almost certainly seen Nami Island — those impossibly straight rows of towering trees lining a golden path, couples walking hand-in-hand, and a dreamy, storybook atmosphere that feels a world away from Seoul's buzzing streets. The good news? It's only about an hour and a half from the city, making it one of the most rewarding and accessible day trips you can do.

Officially called Namisum (나미섬), this half-moon-shaped island in the North Han River is technically its own "nation" — the Naminara Republic — complete with its own passport stamps and currency. It's quirky, it's beautiful, and it's genuinely fun. Whether you're visiting in spring for the cherry blossoms, summer for the lush green canopy, autumn for the fiery foliage, or winter for the snow-dusted lanes, Nami Island delivers every single season.

Think of this guide as advice from a local Korean friend who's done this trip more times than they can count. Let's make sure your visit goes smoothly.

Getting to Nami Island from Seoul: Step-by-Step

There are two main ways to get to Nami Island: by public transit or by organized tour. Both are totally doable — here's the breakdown.

Option 1: Public Transit (The Independent Route)

This is the most flexible and budget-friendly option. Here's the route:

  1. Take the ITX-Cheongchun train or subway to Gapyeong Station. From Seoul Station or Yongsan Station, hop on the ITX-Cheongchun express train to Gapyeong Station (가평역). The ride takes roughly 60–70 minutes and is significantly faster than the regular subway. Alternatively, you can take Subway Line 1 to Cheongnyangni Station and transfer to the ITX there. Seats on the ITX can sell out on weekends, so book in advance through the Korail website or app.
  2. Get to Nami Island Ferry Dock. From Gapyeong Station, you have two options: take a local taxi (around 5–10 minutes, approximately ₩5,000–₩8,000) or hop on the Gapyeong Tour Bus that runs a loop between the station and nearby attractions including the ferry dock.
  3. Take the ferry to the island. The short ferry ride across the river takes only about 5 minutes, but it's a lovely little moment. Ferries run frequently throughout the day.

Total travel time from central Seoul: Approximately 1.5 to 2 hours depending on your starting point and connections.

Option 2: Organized Day Tour

Many tour operators run combined day trips that include Nami Island alongside nearby spots like Petite France or the Garden of Morning Calm. These tours pick you up from central Seoul (often near Myeongdong or Hongdae), handle all the transport logistics, and include entrance fees. They're a great option if you want a hassle-free day or are traveling solo and want to meet other visitors. Expect to pay roughly ₩50,000–₩80,000 per person depending on inclusions.

Tickets and Entry Fees

To enter Nami Island, you need to purchase a ferry ticket + island admission combo. As of 2026, admission is approximately ₩16,000 for adults (round-trip ferry included), though prices can vary slightly by season or for special events — always check the official Namisum website before you go for the most current pricing.

You can buy tickets at the ferry dock ticket booth or, more conveniently, online in advance. Buying online is highly recommended on weekends and public holidays, as queues at the booth can be surprisingly long. The island also accepts major credit cards and mobile payment apps like KakaoPay and Samsung Pay.

What to Do on Nami Island

The island is small enough to explore comfortably on foot in a few hours, but there's more to do here than just walk the famous tree lanes. Here's what to prioritize:

Walk the Iconic Tree-Lined Paths

This is the main event, and it absolutely lives up to the hype. The Metasequoia Lane and Ginkgo Tree Road are the most photographed spots on the island. The towering trees create a natural cathedral effect that's stunning in every season. Go early in the morning if you want photos without crowds — the light is also much better before midday.

Spot the Ostriches and Other Animals

Yes, there are ostriches roaming the island. And rabbits, squirrels, and various birds. It adds to the slightly surreal, fairy-tale atmosphere of the place. Kids absolutely love this part, but even adults find it charming. Keep a respectful distance from the animals and don't try to feed them human food.

Explore the Art Installations and Sculptures

Nami Island takes its identity as a cultural space seriously. You'll find quirky sculptures, murals, and art installations scattered throughout the grounds. Keep your eyes open as you wander — some of the best pieces are tucked away off the main paths.

Rent a Bike

Bike rentals are available on the island and are a fun way to cover more ground quickly. Tandem bikes are especially popular with couples. Rental prices are reasonable (approximately ₩3,000–₩8,000 per hour depending on bike type), and the island's flat terrain makes it easy for all fitness levels.

Visit the Naminara Republic "Customs"

When you arrive on the island, you'll pass through a playful mock "customs" area — you can even get your passport stamped with a Naminara Republic stamp. It's a fun little touch that makes for a great souvenir, especially if you're traveling with a physical passport journal.

Where to Eat on Nami Island

There are several cafés and restaurants on the island, ranging from casual snack stalls to sit-down restaurants. Here are a few things to know:

  • Try the corn dog (hotteok) and other Korean street snacks from the food stalls near the ferry landing — they're freshly made and perfect for eating while you walk.
  • The island restaurants serve Korean staples like bibimbap, doenjang jjigae (fermented soybean paste stew), and grilled meats. Prices are slightly higher than in Seoul, which is typical for tourist destinations.
  • If you're on a tight budget, consider packing your own lunch — there are plenty of beautiful picnic spots under the trees.
  • Grab a coffee or hot chocolate from one of the island's cozy cafés, especially in autumn or winter when the weather turns crisp.

Best Time to Visit Nami Island

Honestly, Nami Island is worth visiting year-round, but each season has its own magic:

  • Spring (March–May): Cherry blossoms and fresh green leaves make this one of the most beautiful times to visit. It's also the busiest — book everything in advance.
  • Summer (June–August): Lush, green, and vibrant. It can be hot and humid, so bring sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • Autumn (September–November): Arguably the most stunning season. The ginkgo and maple trees turn brilliant shades of gold and red. Peak foliage (usually mid-to-late October) draws huge crowds.
  • Winter (December–February): Snow transforms the island into a quiet, magical wonderland. Crowds thin out significantly, and the atmosphere is wonderfully peaceful.

For the best experience, aim to arrive when the island opens in the morning and plan to leave by early afternoon before the day-trip crowds peak.

Etiquette Tips for Visiting Nami Island

Korea is generally a very respectful travel destination, and a few simple etiquette points will go a long way:

  • Don't litter. The island is meticulously maintained — use the bins provided and pack out what you pack in.
  • Be mindful of other photographers. The tree lanes get crowded, and everyone wants their shot. Be patient, take turns, and don't linger in the middle of a path for extended photo sessions when others are waiting.
  • Keep noise levels down. Especially in the quieter garden areas, a calm atmosphere is part of the experience. Loud music from portable speakers is frowned upon.
  • Respect the animals. Don't chase, corner, or feed the resident animals with outside food.
  • Follow signage. Some areas of the island are designated quiet zones or have specific rules — pay attention to posted signs.

Common Mistakes First-Timers Make (And How to Avoid Them)

  • Not booking the ITX train in advance. Weekend trains fill up fast. Book your Gapyeong-bound ITX seats at least a few days ahead, especially during peak seasons.
  • Arriving too late. If you roll up at noon on a Saturday in October, you'll be fighting through massive crowds. Get there early — ideally when the ferry starts running in the morning.
  • Underestimating the return journey. The trip back to Seoul can take longer than expected if you're catching a busy train. Check the return ITX schedule before you leave Seoul so you're not scrambling.
  • Forgetting cash for small purchases. Most places accept cards, but having some Korean won on hand for small snacks or the tour bus is always a good idea.
  • Only visiting Nami Island. Since you're already in Gapyeong, consider combining your trip with a visit to Petite France (a quirky French-themed village) or the Garden of Morning Calm, both nearby. The Gapyeong Tour Bus connects all three.

Combining Nami Island with Other Gapyeong Attractions

Gapyeong County is home to several other popular day-trip destinations that pair perfectly with Nami Island:

  • Petite France (쁘띠프랑스): A colorful French-themed cultural village perched on a hillside overlooking a lake. It's charming, photogenic, and great for kids. About 20 minutes from Nami Island by the tour bus.
  • Garden of Morning Calm (아침고요수목원): A beautifully landscaped botanical garden that's especially magical during its winter light festival (December–February). Allow at least 1.5–2 hours here.
  • Gapyeong Rail Bike: A fun activity where you pedal a four-wheeled bike along a scenic riverside rail track. Great for families and couples.

If you're combining multiple attractions, an organized tour or the Gapyeong Tour Bus day pass will save you time and stress.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I spend on Nami Island?

Most visitors spend 2 to 3 hours on the island, which is enough time to walk all the main paths, grab a bite, and explore the art installations. If you're renting bikes or really soaking in the atmosphere, budget up to 4 hours.

Is Nami Island worth visiting in 2026?

Absolutely. Despite being well-known, the island has maintained its charm and continues to be one of the most genuinely beautiful day trips from Seoul. It's especially worth it if you're visiting during spring or autumn.

Can I visit Nami Island without a tour?

Yes, completely. The public transit route via ITX train to Gapyeong Station is straightforward and well-signposted. Many independent travelers do this trip with no issues at all.

Is Nami Island family-friendly?

Very much so. The flat terrain, roaming animals, bike rentals, and open spaces make it ideal for families with children of all ages.

What should I wear to Nami Island?

Comfortable walking shoes are a must. In autumn and winter, dress in warm layers as it can get cold near the river. In summer, light, breathable clothing and sunscreen are essential. The island is very photogenic, so many visitors wear something a little special for photos — but it's entirely up to you!

Is there Wi-Fi on Nami Island?

There is limited Wi-Fi available in some indoor areas, but don't rely on it. Korean SIM cards and pocket Wi-Fi devices (available for rent at Incheon Airport) work well throughout the island and on the train journey.
