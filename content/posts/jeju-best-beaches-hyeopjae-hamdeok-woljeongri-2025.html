🏖️ Discover Jeju’s Best Beaches — A Paradise for Swimmers and Sun Lovers

Jeju Island is blessed with some of the most stunning beaches in Korea, each offering a unique charm — from emerald waters to soft white sands and vibrant coastal cafés. Whether you’re planning a relaxing day in the sun or a refreshing swim, here are the top beaches you shouldn’t miss in 2025.

🌊 1️⃣ Hyeopjae Beach (협재해변)

Located on Jeju’s northwest coast, Hyeopjae Beach is famous for its clear turquoise water and powdery white sand. The shallow waters and gentle waves make it ideal for families, couples, and beginner swimmers.

  • 🏝️ Best for: Swimming, snorkeling, and sunset walks.
  • 🌅 Don’t miss: The view of Biyangdo Island just offshore — especially magical at sunset.
  • ☕ Nearby cafés: Geomeongtang Café and Sunset Cliff Jeju for ocean views and photo-worthy desserts.

Pro tip: Arrive early in summer, as Hyeopjae is one of Jeju’s most photographed beaches!

🌴 2️⃣ Hamdeok Beach (함덕해수욕장)

Just 30 minutes from Jeju City, Hamdeok Beach is known for its calm, shallow lagoon-like water — perfect for swimming and paddleboarding. The surrounding area features vibrant murals, restaurants, and seaside trails, making it a top choice for both locals and visitors.

  • 🏖️ Best for: Families and beginners who want safe, warm water for swimming.
  • 🌅 Don’t miss: The Hamdeok Seoubong Trail — a short, scenic hike offering panoramic ocean views.
  • 🍽️ Where to eat: Monet Café for brunch, or Doldam Restaurant for fresh Jeju seafood.

Fun fact: Hamdeok Beach glows at night with underwater LED lights, creating a romantic seaside ambiance after sunset.

☕ 3️⃣ Woljeongri Beach (월정리해변)

Woljeongri Beach is Jeju’s café paradise. This east coast gem features soft sand, gentle waves, and an entire street lined with trendy beachfront cafés. It’s less crowded than Hamdeok but equally stunning for swimming, surfing, or relaxing with coffee by the sea.

  • 🌴 Best for: Couples, digital nomads, and beach café lovers.
  • ☕ Top cafés: Café Morebi, Café Delmoondo, and Lazy Beach — all offering panoramic ocean views.
  • 🚲 Bonus tip: Rent a scooter or bicycle and ride along Woljeong Coastal Road at sunset.

Traveler’s tip: Many cafés offer hammocks or bean bags facing the sea — perfect for a lazy afternoon date.

🏄 4️⃣ Other Notable Beaches to Explore

  • Jungmun Beach — Known for stronger waves and water sports like surfing and jet-skiing.
  • Gwakji Beach — Features natural spring water pools by the shore — ideal for cooling off after swimming.
  • Iho Tewoo Beach — Famous for its red and white horse-shaped lighthouses and sunset views.

💡 Final Thoughts

From Hyeopjae’s postcard-perfect lagoon to Hamdeok’s gentle waters and Woljeongri’s café-lined coast, Jeju’s beaches are more than just summer destinations — they’re year-round escapes for anyone craving peace and natural beauty. Each beach has its own character, so explore them all for a full island experience.

In 2025, Jeju continues to enhance its coastal facilities and eco-friendly initiatives, ensuring that every beach remains as pristine as ever for locals and travelers alike.

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

1. Which Jeju beach is best for swimming?

Hamdeok Beach and Hyeopjae Beach are best for safe, shallow swimming with lifeguards on duty during summer months.

2. Are Jeju beaches free to enter?

Yes! Most beaches in Jeju, including Hyeopjae, Hamdeok, and Woljeongri, are free to access. Parking fees may apply during peak season.

3. Can you swim at Jeju beaches year-round?

Swimming season officially runs from June to September, but warm weather in May and October also attracts sunbathers and photographers.

4. Which beach has the best cafés?

Woljeongri Beach is the go-to for café lovers — it’s lined with trendy coffee shops offering ocean views, desserts, and even rooftop seating.
