Planning a winter trip to Jeju Island in 2025? Choosing the right base is crucial, especially when balancing early morning Hallasan snow hikes with relaxed coastal sightseeing. Jeju in winter (December to February) offers dramatic snowy landscapes on the mountain and crisp, beautiful views along the coast.

In this guide, we have selected the top 3 hotels for winter 2025, catering to hikers needing easy mountain access and sightseers looking for luxury and convenience. These selections are based on real data regarding location practicality, winter amenities, and traveler reviews.

Where is the Best Area to Stay in Jeju in Winter?

Generally, **Seogwipo (South)** is slightly warmer and less windy than **Jeju City (North)** in winter. For Hallasan hikers, staying near major bus routes to trailheads (Seongpanak or Gwanneumsa) is vital. For general sightseers, the **Jungmun Tourist Complex** offers luxury and indoor activities, while the **East (near Seongsan)** is great for sunrise views and coastal drives.

1. The Hiker’s Haven (Best for Hallasan Access): Hotel Bridgene Jeju

If your main goal is hiking Hallasan's snowy peak, convenience is key. The **Seongpanak trail** is the most popular winter route. Staying in Seogwipo City near the main bus terminal makes catching the early morning 281 bus to the trailhead incredibly easy.

Why stay here in Winter 2025?

  • Location: Located centrally in Seogwipo. It is a short walk to bus stops that connect directly to the Hallasan Seongpanak trailhead, eliminating the need for an expensive long-distance taxi before dawn.
  • Comfort for Recovery: It offers clean, modern rooms with comfortable beds—essential for recovering after an 8-hour hike.
  • Nearby Amenities: Surrounded by local restaurants and convenience stores to stock up on hiking snacks and enjoy a hearty dinner post-hike.
  • Value: Excellent price-to-quality ratio for budget-conscious hikers.

2. The Luxury Sightseer’s Escape: Parnas Hotel Jeju

For those who want to enjoy Jeju's winter scenery in ultimate comfort, the **Jungmun Tourist Complex** is ideal. **Parnas Hotel Jeju** stands out for its dramatic cliffside location and exceptional winter amenities.

Why stay here in Winter 2025?

  • Heated Infinity Pool: The highlight is the stunning outdoor heated infinity pool, the longest in Korea. Swimming while overlooking the winter ocean is an unforgettable experience.
  • Ocean Views: Almost all areas of the hotel offer breathtaking, panoramic views of the rugged winter coastline.
  • Indoor Attractions: Located in Jungmun, you are close to indoor spots like museums and botanical gardens, perfect for escaping cold windy days.

3. The Trendy Explorer's Base (East Jeju): Playce Camp Jeju

If you want to explore the eastern side of the island, including **Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak)** and nearby **camellia habitats**, **Playce Camp Jeju** offers a unique, vibrant atmosphere targeting younger travelers.

Why stay here in Winter 2025?

  • Proximity to Sunrise: It's located very close to Seongsan Ilchulbong, making it easy to catch the famous New Year's or winter sunrise.
  • Vibrant Atmosphere: More than just a hotel, it features on-site cafes, pubs, and pop-up stores, providing a fun environment even on chilly winter evenings.
  • Winter Camellia Access: A great base for driving to eastern camellia hotspots like Jeju Dongbaek Arboretum.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) About Jeju Winter Stays

Should I stay in Jeju City or Seogwipo in winter?

For a slightly warmer climate and easier access to most tourist attractions, **Seogwipo (South)** is generally preferred in winter. However, **Jeju City (North)** is better for late arrivals near the airport and access to the Gwanneumsa trail on Hallasan.

Do hotels in Jeju have good heating in winter?

Yes, virtually all hotels and guesthouses in Jeju are well-equipped with modern heating systems, including "ondol" (floor heating) or central heating, ensuring a warm stay even during cold spells.

Is it necessary to rent a car for a winter trip?

While highly recommended for flexibility, especially for sightseeing, it's not strictly necessary. If your main focus is hiking Hallasan, staying near a major bus route works well. If driving in winter, requesting snow tires or chains is crucial if heading into the mountains.
