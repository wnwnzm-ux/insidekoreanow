Why Visit Jeju Island in Winter (2025)?

Jeju's winter charm lies in its diverse offerings. You can experience the breathtaking snowy vistas of Hallasan, then descend to warmer coastal areas to witness the vivid red camellias in full bloom. The island is also less crowded than in peak seasons, allowing for a more serene travel experience.

1. Hallasan Snow Hiking: A Winter Wonderland Experience

Is Hallasan Mountain Open for Hiking in Winter?

Yes, Hallasan Mountain is open for hiking in winter, but specific trails and conditions apply. For the 2025 season, the most popular routes for reaching the summit (Baengnokdam Crater Lake) are the Seongpanak Trail and Gwanneumsan Trail. Both require prior reservation.

  • Seongpanak Trail: (~9.6 km, 5 hours one way to summit) – Generally considered easier.
  • Gwanneumsan Trail: (~8.7 km, 5 hours one way to summit) – More challenging but offers dramatic scenery.

What Do I Need for Hallasan Winter Hiking?

Essential gear includes crampons (아이젠), hiking poles, warm layers, waterproof outer shells, gloves, a hat, and plenty of water/snacks. The weather can change rapidly, so preparation is key for a safe and enjoyable experience.

2. Enchanting Camellia Spots in Jeju

Contrast the snowy peaks with Jeju's winter flowers! The island is famous for its vibrant camellia flowers (동백꽃), which bloom from late November to early March.

Best Camellia Viewing Spots for 2025

  • Camellia Hill (동백 포레스트): A large botanical garden dedicated to camellias. Expect beautiful photo zones and diverse varieties. Located near Andeok-myeon.
  • Weoljeong-ri Beach Camellia Path: A lesser-known spot with camellias blooming right next to the famous turquoise waters. Offers a unique seaside backdrop.
  • Jeju Dongbaek Village (제주동백마을): A charming village setting where you can stroll amongst blooming camellias and enjoy local cafes.

3. Other Winter Activities in Jeju (2025)

  • Jeju Tangerine Picking: Enjoy picking fresh, sweet tangerines at various farms.
  • Coastal Drives: The stunning coastal roads, like Aewol Handam Coastal Walk, offer breathtaking winter sea views.
  • Thermal Spas & Hot Springs: Relax in warm waters after a day of exploration.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it worth visiting Jeju Island in winter?

Absolutely! Jeju offers a unique charm in winter with snow hiking on Hallasan, vibrant camellia fields, and fewer crowds. It's a truly unforgettable experience for nature lovers.

What is the weather like in Jeju in January 2025?

January in Jeju is typically cold, with average temperatures ranging from 3°C to 8°C (37°F to 46°F). Expect occasional snow, especially on Hallasan, and chilly winds along the coast. Proper winter attire is essential.

Do I need to rent a car for winter travel in Jeju?

Renting a car (preferably with 4WD or snow chains if you plan to drive towards Hallasan) is highly recommended for flexibility. However, public transport and local taxis are also available for key attractions.
