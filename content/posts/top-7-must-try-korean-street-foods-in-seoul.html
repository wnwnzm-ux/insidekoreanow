🍢 Discover the Soul of Seoul Through Its Street Food

Seoul’s street food scene is a paradise for food lovers. From sweet treats to spicy snacks, every corner offers something mouthwatering. Whether you’re exploring Myeongdong, Hongdae, or Gwangjang Market, here are 7 must-try Korean street foods you can’t miss during your trip.

1️⃣ Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cakes)

These chewy rice cakes simmered in spicy gochujang sauce are a national favorite. The perfect balance of sweet, spicy, and savory makes tteokbokki an irresistible comfort food.

Where to try: Sindang-dong Tteokbokki Town or Myeongdong street stalls.

2️⃣ Hotteok (Sweet Syrup-Filled Pancakes)

A crispy pancake stuffed with brown sugar, cinnamon, and crushed nuts. Hotteok is especially popular in winter — one bite and you’ll be hooked!

Tip: Try green tea or matcha versions at Insadong’s street markets.

3️⃣ Eomuk (Fish Cake Skewers)

Served on skewers with warm broth, eomuk is the ultimate comfort food during cold weather. The broth is free and refilled as much as you like!

Best spots: Gwangjang Market and Namdaemun Market.

4️⃣ Gimbap (Korean Seaweed Rice Rolls)

Often called the Korean version of sushi, gimbap is filled with rice, vegetables, egg, and ham — wrapped in seaweed. Portable, healthy, and delicious.

Try it at: Local convenience stores or Gwangjang Market’s famous gimbap stalls.

5️⃣ Odeng Soup & Tangsuyuk Combo

Pair your fish cake soup with crispy tangsuyuk (sweet and sour pork). It’s a Korean-Chinese favorite fusion combo you’ll find near subway stations and markets.

6️⃣ Tornado Potato

Spiral-cut potato deep-fried on a stick — crunchy, salty, and so fun to eat! Choose your flavor: cheese, onion, or spicy powder for an extra kick.

7️⃣ Bungeoppang (Fish-Shaped Pastry)

This adorable fish-shaped pastry is filled with sweet red bean, custard, or Nutella. Perfect snack while strolling through winter streets.

Hot tip: Pair it with hot corn tea or sweet milk for the ultimate cozy combo.

💡 Final Thoughts

Seoul’s street food isn’t just about eating — it’s about experiencing Korean culture with every bite. Each dish tells a story of flavor, community, and comfort. So next time you’re in Seoul, skip the fancy restaurants and follow the mouthwatering smell of sizzling street stalls!
