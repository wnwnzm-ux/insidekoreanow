🏡 Finding the Perfect Airbnb in Korea 2025

If you’re planning a trip to Korea in 2025, Airbnb is one of the most popular accommodation options for foreign travelers — especially in Seoul. From palaces and street food markets to nightlife and shopping, the city has everything within easy reach. But before booking, it’s important to know which areas are best and what legal rules apply to Airbnb stays in Korea.

🗺️ Best Airbnb Locations in Seoul for Foreign Visitors

Foreign tourists tend to stay in central Seoul — where sightseeing, transportation, and cultural experiences are easily accessible. Here are the most recommended districts for your next trip:

🏯 Gyeongbokgung Area

Perfect for travelers who love Korean history. Many Airbnb hosts operate near Gyeongbokgung Palace, offering traditional hanok stays with modern touches. You’ll be steps away from famous spots like Bukchon Hanok Village and Insadong.

🛍️ Myeongdong & Sogong-dong

Myeongdong is one of the most foreigner-friendly areas in Seoul — surrounded by landmarks such as Namdaemun, Gyeongbokgung, Deoksugung, and Namsan Tower. Subway access is excellent, and most hosts offer English-speaking support. Great for shopping and first-time visitors.

🎨 Hongdae & Sinchon

If you want to experience Seoul’s youthful side, Hongdae and Sinchon are must-visits. Close to Hongik University, these areas are full of guesthouses, indie cafés, and nightlife. It’s a favorite among budget travelers and students.

🕍 Jongno & Dongdaemun

These districts blend Seoul’s history with local charm. You’ll be near Dongdaemun Market, Gwangjang Market, and UNESCO-listed palaces like Changdeokgung. Keep in mind that average Airbnb rates here tend to be higher due to location demand.

💎 Gangnam, Itaewon & Yongsan

For luxury seekers, Gangnam and Apgujeong offer upscale stays and high-end shopping. Meanwhile, Itaewon, Hannam, Haebangchon, and Yongsan are the heart of the foreign community — filled with international restaurants and multicultural vibes. About 62% of Seoul’s Airbnb listings are concentrated in Yongsan District.

🌊 Other Popular Cities for Airbnb in Korea

  • Jeju Island: Korea’s largest Airbnb market, offering scenic ocean views and strong income potential for hosts. The average monthly revenue per room is around ₩1.9 million, with over 50% occupancy.
  • Gangneung & Sokcho: Coastal cities just a few hours from Seoul. With over 3,000 listings, these destinations are perfect for summer vacations.
  • Busan: A vibrant seaside city with roughly 2,500 listings and a 37% occupancy rate — offering both city life and beaches.
  • Jeonju: Known for its Hanok Village, with about 1,000 Airbnb stays blending tradition and comfort.

🚨 Legal Requirements & Safety Tips for Airbnb Guests

While Airbnb is widely used in Korea, the country enforces strict laws under “Urban Guesthouse Business for Foreign Tourists”. If you’re a foreign visitor, here’s what you should know before booking:

  • Foreign-Only Stays: Only foreigners are legally allowed to stay in urban guesthouses registered under this system. Koreans cannot book these accommodations unless in special pilot regions (e.g., Gangneung, Jeonju).
  • Host Residency Required: The host must live in the property and offer basic cultural experiences or foreign-language support.
  • Building Restrictions: Only detached houses, multi-family homes, or small apartments under 230m² can be used. Officetels and studio rooms are not allowed.
  • Safety Standards: Fire alarms, extinguishers, evacuation maps, and emergency lights are required by law. Buildings over 3 stories must have escape equipment installed.
  • Registration Requirement: As of October 2024, Airbnb requires hosts to upload business registration certificates. Existing hosts must comply by October 16, 2025, or risk listing removal by 2026.

💬 Note: Guests are not penalized for staying at an unregistered property, but they may face difficulties if legal issues arise — such as cancellation disputes or safety concerns.

📊 Market Trends & Guest Satisfaction

As of March 2024, Seoul had around 10,687 active Airbnb listings, recovering to nearly 90% of pre-pandemic levels. Popular districts include Mapo, Jung, Jongno, and Gangnam. In 2022, Airbnb transactions in Seoul reached ₩150.7 billion across 590,000 bookings — showing a steady market rebound.

However, guests report that cleanliness, communication, and check-in experience can vary. Always read reviews carefully before booking.

💡 Final Thoughts

Staying in an Airbnb in Korea is one of the best ways to experience local culture and live like a Seoulite. Choose neighborhoods close to public transport, double-check legal compliance, and you’ll have a safe, authentic stay. Whether it’s a hanok near Gyeongbokgung or a rooftop apartment in Itaewon, Korea’s Airbnb scene in 2025 is more exciting and diverse than ever.

💬 Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can foreigners legally use Airbnb in Korea?

Yes, foreigners can use Airbnb in Korea through licensed “Urban Guesthouse for Foreign Tourists.” However, most are for foreign guests only, and hosts must live in the property.

2. Which area is best for Airbnb stays in Seoul?

For first-time visitors, Myeongdong and Hongdae are ideal due to their access to shopping, nightlife, and public transport. For culture lovers, Gyeongbokgung and Jongno offer traditional hanok experiences.

3. Are there restrictions on Airbnb for Koreans?

Yes. In most areas, Koreans cannot stay in foreign-only Airbnb accommodations. Only a few cities like Gangneung or Jeonju allow limited domestic stays under special permits.

4. What should I check before booking?

Check if the host is registered under “Urban Guesthouse for Foreign Tourists,” read recent reviews, and confirm amenities such as heating, air conditioning, and fire safety devices.
