🔥 Discover the Ultimate Korean BBQ Experience in Seoul

Seoul is home to some of the best Korean BBQ restaurants in the world — from Michelin-recognized steakhouses to cozy local joints beloved by celebrities. For foreign visitors, Korean BBQ offers more than just grilled meat — it’s an immersive dining culture that blends smoky aromas, shared dishes, and lively atmosphere. Here’s your curated guide to the top Korean BBQ spots in Seoul that will make your trip unforgettable.

🍖 Michelin Guide-Recommended Korean BBQ

These restaurants are praised by the Michelin Guide for their top-quality meats, expert grilling, and authentic Korean flavors.

🥩 Premium Beef Restaurants

  • Beodeunamu House (버드나무집) – Since 1977, this legendary restaurant has specialized in 1++ grade Korean beef (Hanwoo). The marinated galbi and jumulleok (lightly seasoned beef) are grilled over charcoal for a smoky finish. Their signature salt-seasoned beef ribs — with no soy sauce — highlight the meat’s pure, buttery flavor.
  • Gombawi (곰바위) – Serving premium cuts like yang (tripe) and daechang (beef intestines) for over 30 years, this Samsung-dong gem is famous for its Baekyang-gui. From ribeye to brisket, every cut is melt-in-your-mouth perfection.
  • Gukbinkan Tonguidong (통의동 국빈관) – Set in a traditional hanok house, this restaurant offers a peaceful dining experience with Hanwoo T-bone steak and charcoal-grilled marinated ribs. Enjoy your meal while admiring the serene courtyard views.

🐔 Chicken BBQ

  • Semigye (세미계) – Located in Hannam-dong, this charcoal-grilled chicken restaurant offers tender, lightly pre-grilled chicken paired with cheese fondue and tortilla wraps. Try the rare cuts like chicken neck or thigh for a gourmet twist on Korea’s beloved dakgalbi.

🐷 Pork BBQ

  • Geum Dwaeji Sikdang (금돼지식당) – Situated in Sindang-dong, this stylish spot with white tiles and gold signage draws long lines daily. It serves YBD pork (Yorkshire, Berkshire, Duroc crossbreed) for premium texture and juiciness. The bone-in samgyeopsal and neck cuts grilled on hot charcoal are simply divine.

🐑 Lamb BBQ

  • Ichiryu (이치류) – Korea’s first Sapporo-style lamb BBQ restaurant, known for using only fresh Australian lamb under one year old. The staff grills the meat tableside, making it an approachable choice even for first-time lamb eaters.

🐙 Seafood BBQ

  • Chungmuro Jjukkumi Bulgogi (충무로 쭈꾸미 불고기) – Since 1976, this spot has specialized in charcoal-grilled spicy baby octopus. The signature sauce — called the “magic sauce” — is addictively savory and spicy. The assorted combo with scallops and shellfish is a must-try.

🌟 More Korean BBQ Spots Loved by Locals

📍 Hongdae Area

  • Cheonggiwa Galbi (청기와갈비) – Just two minutes from Hongdae Station Exit 1, this long-standing BBQ restaurant offers valet parking and a refined interior. Their Hanwoo short ribs are best enjoyed lightly grilled with sea salt — a true melt-in-your-mouth experience.
  • Don Blanc (돈블랑) – A popular Hongdae pork BBQ spot loved by locals and tourists alike, known for its thick-cut samgyeopsal and modern dining vibe.
  • Wonjo Sutbul Sogeumgui (원조 숯불소금구이) – Located near Hwayang Intersection, this retro-style restaurant offers a cozy atmosphere with authentic charcoal-grilled meats that capture the essence of traditional Korean BBQ.

📍 Other Popular Districts

  • Choego Galbi (최고갈비 등촌역본점) – A favorite among returning tourists who crave authentic Korean marinated ribs.
  • Montbri (몽블리 명동) – Famous for its all-you-can-eat beef and pork BBQ infused with rich charcoal aroma — the perfect dinner spot after shopping in Myeongdong.
  • Mapo Jip (마포집 강남) – A true “local’s favorite,” known for its pork ribs and lively after-work atmosphere. Expect an authentic Korean dining scene filled with chatter and soju glasses clinking.

💡 Korean BBQ Tips for Visitors

When enjoying Korean BBQ, it’s not just about the meat — the side dishes, lettuce wraps, and sauces complete the experience. Pair each bite with fresh garlic, ssamjang, and kimchi. To finish your meal like a local, order a bowl of doenjang jjigae (soybean stew) or cold naengmyeon noodles.

Korean BBQ in Seoul isn’t just a meal — it’s a celebration of flavor, culture, and togetherness.
