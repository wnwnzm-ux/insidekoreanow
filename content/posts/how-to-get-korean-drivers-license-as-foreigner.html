Getting a Korean Driver's License as a Foreigner: Your Complete 2026 Guide

So you've decided to drive in Korea — great choice! While Korea's public transportation is genuinely world-class, having a local driver's license opens up a whole new side of the country: weekend road trips through the mountains, coastal drives along the East Sea, and the freedom to explore rural areas that buses simply don't reach. The process of getting a Korean driver's license as a foreigner can feel intimidating at first, but with the right preparation it's very manageable. Think of this guide as advice from a friend who's already been through it.

First Things First: Do You Even Need a Korean License?

Before diving in, it's worth asking whether you actually need a full Korean license or whether an International Driving Permit (IDP) will do the job. If you're visiting Korea as a tourist, an IDP issued in your home country — combined with your original national license — is valid for driving in Korea for up to one year from your date of entry. However, once you become a resident (i.e., you hold an Alien Registration Card, or ARC), the IDP is no longer valid. At that point, you'll need either a Korean license or a converted license. This guide covers both paths.

Two Main Routes to a Korean Driver's License

Route 1: License Conversion (Exchange)

If you already hold a valid driver's license from your home country, you may be eligible to convert it directly into a Korean license without taking a driving skills test — and in some cases, without a written test either. This is by far the easier and faster path, and it's available to citizens of countries that have a reciprocal agreement with Korea.

Countries whose licenses can be converted with minimal testing include most EU nations, the United States, Canada, Australia, Japan, and many others. The list is updated periodically, so always confirm your country's status with the Road Traffic Authority (도로교통공단, or TS) or the nearest Driver's License Examination Office before you go.

For a straightforward conversion, you'll generally need to:

  • Prove your license is valid and was issued in your home country (not obtained while living in Korea)
  • Prove you were legally residing in the issuing country when the license was granted
  • Pass a basic vision test at the examination office
  • Submit the required documents (see below)

Some nationalities may still need to pass the written knowledge test even under the conversion route, depending on the bilateral agreement. Check in advance so you're not caught off guard.

Route 2: Applying from Scratch

If your country doesn't have a reciprocal agreement with Korea, or if your foreign license has expired, you'll need to go through the full Korean licensing process. This involves a written test, a driving skills test on a closed course, and a road driving test. It sounds like a lot, but plenty of foreigners pass on their first or second attempt with proper study.

Documents You'll Need

Regardless of which route you're taking, gather these documents before heading to the examination office. Missing even one can mean a wasted trip.

  • Your foreign driver's license (original) — must be valid
  • Official translation of your foreign license — required if it's not in English or Korean. Translations must be done by a certified translator or your country's embassy in Korea.
  • Alien Registration Card (ARC) — proof of residency in Korea
  • Passport — bring the original, not a copy
  • Proof of address in your home country at the time of license issuance — this is often the trickiest document. An old utility bill, bank statement, or official government letter showing your home address during the period you held the license can work. Your embassy may also issue a certificate of residency.
  • Passport-sized photos — typically 3.5cm x 4.5cm; you can get these taken at photo booths near most examination offices for around ₩5,000–₩8,000
  • Application fee — fees are generally modest (roughly ₩10,000–₩20,000 range for the license itself, with additional fees for tests if applicable). Bring cash as well as a card just in case.

Where to Go: Driver's License Examination Offices

The Road Traffic Authority (TS) operates Driver's License Examination Offices (운전면허시험장) across the country. Major cities like Seoul, Busan, Daegu, Incheon, and Daejeon all have dedicated facilities. In Seoul, the most foreigner-friendly offices are in Dobong (도봉) and Gangnam (강남), both of which have English-speaking staff or at least English-language support materials.

You can also handle license conversions at some Police Stations (경찰서) that have traffic divisions, which can be more convenient if you live far from a major examination center. However, full new-license testing must be done at an official examination office.

Getting there: Most examination offices are accessible by subway and bus. For example, the Dobong Examination Office is a short walk from Dobong Station on Seoul Metro Line 1 and 7. Always check the TS website or Naver Maps for the most current directions before you go, as bus routes and exits can change.

The Written Knowledge Test

If you need to take the written test (필기시험), don't panic — it's multiple choice and you only need a score of 60 out of 100 to pass. The test covers Korean traffic laws, road signs, and safe driving practices. It's available in English, Chinese, Vietnamese, and several other languages at most major examination offices.

The best way to prepare is to use the official TS practice test app or website, which has a question bank that closely mirrors the real exam. Spend a few evenings going through the practice questions and you should be well prepared. Most people who study for even 5–10 hours find the test very manageable.

Common Mistakes on the Written Test

  • Confusing Korean speed limits — the default urban speed limit is 50 km/h (reduced from 60 km/h in recent years), and school zones are 30 km/h
  • Misreading questions about right-of-way at intersections — read carefully, as the scenarios can be tricky
  • Skipping the road sign section — Korean signs follow international conventions mostly, but a few are unique

The Driving Skills Test (Closed Course)

If you're going through the full licensing process, after passing the written test you'll need to pass a closed-course driving skills test (기능시험). This is conducted entirely within the examination office's test track — you won't be on public roads yet. The course tests your ability to handle basic maneuvers: starting and stopping smoothly, navigating a curved road, and a parallel parking or T-shaped road section depending on the course layout.

You can rent a practice vehicle at the examination center for a fee, or enroll in a driving academy (자동차학원) to practice beforehand. Many foreigners find a few sessions at a driving academy well worth the investment, as the instructors know the exact course layout and scoring criteria.

The Road Driving Test

The final hurdle is the road driving test (도로주행시험), conducted on actual public roads around the examination office. You'll drive a designated route with an examiner in the passenger seat. The examiner scores you on lane discipline, signaling, speed management, and overall safety. You need 70 out of 100 points to pass.

Tips for the road test:

  • Signal early and clearly — examiners deduct points for late or missing signals
  • Check your mirrors and blind spots visibly (make your head movements obvious)
  • Don't exceed the speed limit even slightly — speed cameras are common and the examiner will note it
  • Come to a complete stop at all stop lines, even if the road looks clear

After You Pass: Receiving Your Korean License

Once you've cleared all required steps, your Korean driver's license is typically issued on the same day or within a few days. It looks like a credit card and includes your photo, name (in both English and Korean if you request it), and license class. Keep it safe — replacing a lost license involves another visit to the examination office and a small fee.

Your Korean license is valid for 10 years for drivers under 65, after which renewal is required. Renewal involves a vision check and, for older drivers, additional health screenings.

Practical Tips for Foreigners

  • Book an appointment online — the TS website allows you to reserve a test slot in advance, which saves you from long walk-in queues, especially on weekdays.
  • Bring a Korean-speaking friend if possible — while English support has improved significantly, having a bilingual friend can smooth over any paperwork confusion.
  • Get your documents certified early — embassy appointments for residency certificates can take a week or more. Don't leave this to the last minute.
  • Check the TS website before you go — office hours and procedures can change, and the official site (koroad.or.kr) has an English section.
  • Don't drive on your foreign license after getting your ARC — this is a common mistake that can result in fines or insurance complications.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I drive in Korea with just my home country's license?

Yes, but only as a tourist and only with a valid International Driving Permit (IDP) alongside your original license. Once you have an Alien Registration Card (ARC), you must obtain a Korean license or convert your foreign one.

How long does the license conversion process take?

If your documents are all in order, a straightforward conversion can be completed in a single visit of a few hours. If you need to take a written test, plan for a second visit. Getting all your documents together beforehand is the biggest time factor.

Is the written test available in English?

Yes. The written knowledge test is available in English at major examination offices, as well as in Chinese, Vietnamese, and several other languages. Confirm availability at your chosen office when booking.

Do I need to surrender my foreign license?

In most cases, yes. When converting your foreign license to a Korean one, the examination office will retain your original foreign license and send it to your home country's licensing authority. Make sure you have a certified copy before submitting the original.

What happens to my Korean license if I leave Korea?

Your Korean driver's license remains valid even if you leave Korea, as long as it hasn't expired. Many countries also accept a Korean license for conversion purposes, so it can be a useful document to hold onto.

Can I use a driving academy to prepare for the tests?

Absolutely, and it's highly recommended if you're going through the full licensing process. Driving academies (자동차학원) offer structured lessons, practice on the exact test course, and instructors who know the scoring criteria inside out. The cost varies but is generally a worthwhile investment compared to failing and retaking tests.

Getting your Korean driver's license as a foreigner takes a bit of paperwork and patience, but it's completely doable — and the freedom it gives you to explore Korea on your own terms is absolutely worth it. Good luck, and drive safe out there! 🚗
